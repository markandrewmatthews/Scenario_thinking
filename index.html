Q1 — Scenario
Input cost shock
The disruption
A combination of barley supply tightness, aluminium price increases, local currency depreciation against the USD, and fuel and logistics disruption is driving significant input cost pressure across your market. Your procurement team is reassessing the AP cost base and signalling upward revision. The full extent of the impact is not yet confirmed.
Severity levels — one will materialise
Base
0% vs AP
Input costs in line with plan. No revision required.
Downside
−5% vs AP
Costs 5% above plan. Operating Profit at risk: approx. €9M — nearly 30% of AP. Marketing budget reallocation can offset this; pricing action is optional but accelerates recovery.
Crisis
−20% vs AP
Costs 20% above plan. Operating Profit at risk: approx. €36M — exceeds full-year AP. Marketing budget alone cannot offset. Pricing action above plan is required to avoid a loss-making position.
Market intelligence
  • Procurement reports spot barley prices up 18% year-on-year in key sourcing origins, with no near-term relief from futures curves.
  • The Arelia birr has depreciated 12% against the USD over the past 60 days, increasing the cost of all USD-denominated imports including aluminium and hops.
  • The market leader has not communicated pricing changes to trade partners. Distributors suggest they are watching the situation before acting.
  • Local teams indicate current signals point toward a prudent assumption between the downside and crisis case. The environment may deteriorate further before it stabilises.

Q3 — Scenario
Competitor strikes — price war
The disruption
Intelligence confirms the market leader is executing a price reduction on Meridian Gold, their largest mainstream SKU. The move is deliberate and trade-facing — key accounts have already been briefed. The quantum of the cut has not been disclosed. Your premium-weighted portfolio is now exposed to a widening price gap in the mainstream segment. Your distributors are waiting for your response.
Severity levels — one will materialise
Base
−2% cut
A limited, tactical move. The competitor tests trade response with a shallow discount. Price gap to your portfolio widens but remains manageable.
Downside
−5% cut
A meaningful value move. The price gap to your portfolio is now significant — mid-range SKUs face direct competitive pressure in key accounts.
Crisis
−10% cut
An aggressive, sustained reduction. The price gap is structural. Holding your current RSP risks delisting of mid-range SKUs and accelerated volume loss.
Market intelligence
  • Distributor contacts report the competitor's commercial team has briefed key accounts to expect a significant value move on Meridian Gold within the next 10 days. The quantum has not been disclosed.
  • Three major retail chains have requested updated commercial proposals from your sales team within 30 days — a direct signal that your portfolio's value positioning is under review.
  • Your commercial team assesses that a price gap above 8% vs Meridian Gold is the threshold at which mainstream consumers begin to switch at scale.
  • Group Strategy has signalled market share defence as a priority — but within margin guardrails. No blanket approval for unilateral price cuts has been granted by the Group CFO.
